The Star

The Star is the central star of the Caisa Planetary System.
Upon the arrival of CAAI in the year 2355 AD, the Star was
believed to be a white dwarf, a remnant of a dead star that
existed eons before humans were even recorded to have existed.

However, after centuries of Isan development and expansion
across the Caisa Planetary System, the Star has been observed to
grow in size and shift in hue. According to the human records and
observations done by experienced Astronomeers, the growth of the
Star is at an abnormally high rate when compared to other stars
within the Parumlac (Lechita) Quadrant of the Milky Way Galaxy.

It is hypothesized that the Star may be a unique type of
star referred to as a quark star. Little is known about these
previously only theoretical celestial bodies, other than that
they are so dense that the pressure within breaks down subatomic
particles and allows their quarks to roam free, creating a makeup
of which is called quark matter.

The rapid expansion of the Star, however, is only abnormal
when compared to the lifecycle of other observed stars and is no
cause for alarm. It is unknown whether quark stars are capable of
becoming super-massive stars or even going supernova, but when
projecting the typical lifecycle of a star onto the Star at its
current rate of growth, a supernova event is still calculated to
not occur until at least 980 million years from now.
